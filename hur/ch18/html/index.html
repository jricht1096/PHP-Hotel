About Our Hotel

Here at Gingers Paradise we understand the horrors that a ginger can endure this day and age. We know of the one true enemy all gingers face, the thing we can never defeat but can only hide from. Thats why our founding ginger fathers, made the trek as far up north as they couldve possible managed to a place were the cold is so powerful that it beats the heat. They thus created this safe haven against the Sun. Join us at the one true place a ginger belongs. join us at the one true place a ginger is safe.

Non-Ginger Policies

Our wonderful hotel is not only for those of the redheads, we may be a safe haven but we still serve as a typical hotel for anyone to attend. We do not discriminate against the "normies" here at Gingers Paradise, but do be warned there are a few services that only a premium ginger may obtain. The only way a "normie" or a "Day walker", people with red hair but no light skin, can obtain premium ginger status is with the small small fee of their soul and perhaps a ritual here and there. After this process is complete premium ginger status is obtained and the anti-tanning beds and water fountains may then be accessed.

Travel options

Our beautiful and beloved hotel can indeed be hard to access. The North pole isnt visited often by people but do not fret our founding ginger fathers have prepared for just about everything. The hotel is of course connected to the underground railways that every ginger was of course taught about in ginger initiation on their 7th birthday, although we are very familiar with the fact that "normies" do not have access to the railway. Seeing as planes do not make flights this far up north, we have created an alternative means of travel for our ginger-impaired guests. We have negotiated with good Ole Saint Nick and he was able to spare us a sleigh and a few reigndeer. Upon A "normies" reservation on our website we will dispatch a sleigh to their location which will be found via IP address. The sleigh will also be used to transfer you back after your stay.
